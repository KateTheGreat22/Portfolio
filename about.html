About Me!

I'm Kaitlyn Johnson: a writer, educator, and advocate for the transformative power of literature.

How I Got Here

I grew up in a small Arizona town where higher education was not common and where only 17% of adults held college degrees. Pursuing a college degree was often discouraged. However, a handful of community college professors saw something in me and fought alongside me for my education. That experience formed in me a deep belief that everyone deserves access to learning, and that literature has the power to change lives.

My passion for making literature accessible took root at my local public library, where I noticed the classical fiction section gathering dust and youth programs dwindling. As a library clerk, I advocated for adding classic literary works to our collection and took over the youth and children's programming. Within months, our Children's Storytime grew from three attendees to over fifteen each week and continued to grow. I then launched the TEEN, STEM, and LEGO Clubs that are still running today. One of my proudest moments was watching a teenager check out a classical novel I'd recommended for our library circulation, which was proof that when we make great literature available, people reach for it.

My Prospected Academic Goals

My goal is simple: to become the kind of English professor who advocates for students the way mine advocated for me, to create spaces where everyone feels confident in their ability to communicate, and to keep great literature in rotation and accessible to all. I believe that every individual has their own way of communicating, their own story to tell, and their own contribution to make to the larger global conversation.
